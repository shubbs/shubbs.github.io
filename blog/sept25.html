Nighttime thoughts by the water

September 25, 2015

Music: Golden Hour by Broke For Free

The ocean does not solve problems, but in water, problems seem to weigh less.

Each drop that washes to shore is a reminder that we come and go. The sounds of waves and wind whisk away the voices in my head that try to keep this a secret.

The waters have no care for your ambition, your success, your manipulative manoeuvres.

The ocean in and of itself is enough. There is no need for a sunset, sunrise, or another person; these can be nice, but peace comes from within and the ocean leads you there. Looking out at a blank sky that meets a blank ocean, where else is there to look but inward?

We need these waters in our lives more often, gifting us continual reminders to be better, more genuine and thoughtful people.

Fame is a fickle food

Emily Dickinson, 1830 - 1886

Fame is a fickle food
Upon a shifting plate
Whose table once a
Guest but not
The second time is set.

Whose crumbs the crows inspect
And with ironic caw
Flap past it to the Farmer’s Corn –
Men eat of it and die.
